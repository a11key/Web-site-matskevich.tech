Battery Tracking System “Smart Battery”

Domain: Embedded Systems, Battery Management Systems
Project Type: Experimental Development
Year: 2024–present
Role: Sole Developer

Technical Summary

A data logger was designed to collect battery voltage, current, and temperature under real load conditions for model validation and parameterization.

Device iterations:

  1. Initial real-time monitor – dual-unit system with wireless transmission to PC.
  2. Single-unit microSD logger – onboard storage, sampling up to 30 Hz.
  3. High-power adaptation – ranges 200 A / 100 V for electric buggy testing.
  4. PCB-based version – 6 deployable units with SoC estimation via Coulomb counting and EKF.

Key Achievements

  • Sampling rate increased tenfold while maintaining accuracy: Temp ±0.5 °C, Voltage ±0.1 %, Current ±2 %.
  • Scaled from prototype to 10 deployable units.
  • Optimized data logging: 7.3 KB/sec; 16 GB microSD supports 27 days continuous recording.

Impact / Outcome

  • Validated UAV and electric buggy battery models under real conditions.
  • Provided high-resolution data for accurate battery parameterization.
  • Supplied measurements for reliable SoC estimation in the field.

Ion Wind Thruster

Domain: Plasma Physics, High-Voltage Systems, Electrohydrodynamics
Project Type: Applied Research + Experimental Development
Year: 2024
Role: Experimental Systems Designer and Theoretical Model Developer

Technical Summary

Investigated ion wind generated by corona discharge. Developed a theoretical model of thrust for a specific form factor. Demonstrated thrust depends linearly on electrode spacing and current.

Designed and constructed experimental setup to validate the theoretical model. Explored the dependency of minimal corona discharge voltage on electrode curvature.

Key Achievements

  • Derived empirical relationship between thrust, electrode spacing, and current.
  • Validated theoretical formula with 10 % accuracy.
  • Investigated minimal corona voltage dependency on electrode curvature.

Impact / Outcome

  • Won the MIPT Competition of Student Projects in Physics (2024, 2025).
